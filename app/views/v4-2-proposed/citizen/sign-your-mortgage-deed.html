Sign your mortgage deed

Use this service to check your mortgage details are correct, and sign your mortgage deed. Your conveyancer will then ensure the Land Registry is updated.

To do this you will need:

  • To verify your identity using GOV.UK Verify

Start now

Any information you provide during this process will be stored by GOV.UK for fraud prevention purposes.

Why is the government involved in this?

The Land Registry registers any interests affecting registered land, such as a change of mortgage. Find out more here.

If this page will be generally available on GOV.UK...

it doesn't explain itself to people who arrive on the page without guidance i.e. whether they should be seeking to transact, or not;

There’s nothing that outlines criteria or reasons why users do or don't need to go through the transaction;

There needs to be a clear understanding on whether they're at the right page or not [or if they're at the right page just too early to transact];

There needs to be a clear articulation on the audience for that page. For example, “use this page if you're in the middle of this process and your conveyancer has asked you to do it” – explaining to a curious user how they could check if their conveyancer has prepared their details. And why this page might not be right for a user;

Is it appropriate to giving timing guidance to the user – for example, please try to sign your mortgage within 1 week of receiving the link?
